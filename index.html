Documentation · Shadow Weaver Yield Strategy

Capital‑Protected Funding Arbitrage

Shadow Weaver is a market‑neutral yield vault that captures funding rate premiums on Drift Protocol while enforcing a hard on‑chain loss limit, built for the Ranger Earn ecosystem.

Chain: Solana Devnet
Venue: Drift Protocol PERPs
Style: Market‑neutral funding capture

The strategy allows users to deposit USDC into an institutional‑style vault which opens delta‑neutral positions on Drift and harvests funding payments, while a 15% maximum loss is mathematically enforced at the smart‑contract level.

Strategy Thesis

Shadow Weaver is built around a simple but powerful thesis: in perpetual futures markets, traders routinely pay funding to express directional conviction, while a neutral counterparty can earn this funding as yield. The vault steps into that neutral role and collects funding premiums without betting on price direction.

The strategy focuses on markets where short positions are structurally compensated via positive funding—currently the SOL perpetual market on Drift. By combining a neutral exposure profile with strict drawdown guardrails, the vault aims to generate equity‑like returns from a yield stream that is largely orthogonal to spot price trends.

Core return driver

Funding payments on perpetual swaps, not price appreciation, constitute the primary P&L engine. Long‑horizon backtests on live funding data indicate the potential for annualized yields above 100% under recent market conditions, subject to funding and liquidity regimes.

Risk philosophy

Instead of relying solely on off‑chain bots to stop losses, risk limits are baked into the program logic: the vault expresses how much it can lose in code, then lets the execution layer optimize how much it can earn within that sandbox.

How It Works in Practice

Lifecycle of a deposit

When a user deposits USDC into the Shadow Weaver vault, the Solana program locks that capital on‑chain and records the depositor’s share in the vault. The program then issues a cross‑program invocation (CPI) to Drift to open or update an institutional trading account associated with the vault.

From the user’s perspective, this is a single action: send USDC to the vault and receive a claim on the strategy’s performance. From the system’s perspective, the deposit expands the vault’s risk capacity and allows larger market‑neutral positions to be deployed on Drift.

Execution by the Shadow Weaver bot

An off‑chain TypeScript executor, the Shadow Weaver bot, continuously streams market and funding data from Drift using the Drift SDK and lightweight RPC calls. It monitors funding rates, volatility conditions, and vault utilization to decide when to enter, resize, or close positions.

When an attractive opportunity is detected—typically a sustained positive funding environment on SOL perpetuals—the bot simulates a delta‑neutral allocation that fits within the on‑chain risk budget. It then submits trades via the vault’s Drift account so that the vault earns funding while remaining broadly hedged against directional price movements.

Redemption

Upon a withdrawal request, the bot unwinds outstanding positions on Drift to realize P&L, then the program settles the vault state and returns the user’s proportional share of USDC. All accounting and loss‑cap checks are enforced inside the Solana program before any funds leave the vault.

Risk Management

Risk is enforced at the smart contract layer and reflected in the execution logic. The primary control is a hard maximum loss constraint, expressed as a percentage of vault equity and encoded directly in the Rust program.

If cumulative realized plus unrealized losses on the vault’s Drift positions reach this threshold, the program enters a protective state: further risk‑increasing actions are blocked, the executor is forced to de‑risk, and the vault preserves remaining capital for depositors.

Hard risk limit

Parameter Value Scope
Maximum loss 15% Vault equity (realized + unrealized)
Market SOL perpetuals Drift Protocol

This limit is immutable at runtime: it is not configurable by users or bots and can only be changed via a new program deployment. That design choice mirrors institutional risk systems where drawdown budgets are structural, not ad‑hoc.

Operational safeguards

Infrastructure & connectivity

The executor reads funding and market data via a direct, thin integration with the venue’s infrastructure rather than relying exclusively on congested public RPC endpoints. This reduces the likelihood that risk checks fail or stall during high‑stress market conditions.

The vault is explicitly designed to fail safe: if drift account updates, price feeds, or funding data become unreliable, the system prefers to pause risk‑taking rather than continue trading without accurate inputs.

Position Sizing

Position sizing is derived from vault equity, the 15% maximum loss constraint, and recent market volatility. The goal is to size positions such that a plausible but severe adverse move remains inside the on‑chain loss budget, while still deploying enough notional to meaningfully monetize funding.

In internal simulations, a notional base of 100,000 USDC with the current parameters provides a practical scale: large enough to be relevant on Drift order books, yet small enough that the 15% cap translates to a well‑defined risk window that the strategy must respect at all times.

Sizing constraints (high‑level)

Maximum portfolio loss: 15% of equity Capital baseline (simulations): 100k USDC Venue: Drift SOL‑PERP

The executor will never intentionally open a position whose projected worst‑case loss under configured scenarios would exceed the remaining loss budget. This turn the on‑chain cap into a live risk budget for the bot.

Rebalancing Logic

Rebalancing has two main objectives: keep the portfolio approximately delta‑neutral and ensure that deployed notional remains consistent with the current loss budget and funding environment. The bot periodically re‑evaluates hedge ratios and funding spreads, then adjusts positions accordingly.

In practice, that means: closing or reducing positions when funding collapses or turns negative; resizing exposure when volatility changes the maximum tolerable notional; and re‑centering hedges around the current spot level to keep the strategy focused on funding, not direction.

Rebalancing triggers (conceptual)

Funding regime changes (e.g., positive → flat/negative) Volatility shocks that tighten the loss budget Material drift from target delta‑neutrality

While the exact heuristics live in the off‑chain execution logic, every rebalance is constrained by the same on‑chain invariant: after any adjustment, the vault must still sit within its 15% drawdown envelope.

System Architecture

The system is split into a deterministic on‑chain risk core and a flexible off‑chain execution engine. This separation allows the program to guarantee capital protection and accounting, while the bot can evolve to incorporate more advanced execution and venue logic over time.

On‑chain (Solana · Anchor · Rust)

The Solana program manages vault state, deposits, withdrawals, and loss tracking. It performs CPI calls into Drift to open and maintain the vault’s institutional trading account and enforces all invariants around the 15% loss limit.

Off‑chain (TypeScript · Drift SDK)

The Shadow Weaver bot runs in Node.js using the Drift SDK and Solana Web3 libraries. It subscribes to market feeds, computes funding‑driven opportunities, enforces position‑sizing rules, and submits trades via the vault’s Drift account.

Judge Runbook · Reproducing the Strategy

This section summarizes the minimal steps required for a technical judge to clone, deploy, and observe the strategy on Solana Devnet.

1. Clone and install

# Clone repository
git clone https://github.com/Vinisilva0010/ranger_drift_vault.git
cd ranger_drift_vault

# Install Node/TypeScript dependencies
npm install @solana/web3.js @drift-labs/sdk @coral-xyz/anchor ts-node typescript

2. Build and deploy the Anchor program

# Compile and deploy the vault program to Devnet
anchor build
anchor deploy

3. Initialize Devnet vault infrastructure

ANCHOR_PROVIDER_URL="https://api.devnet.solana.com" \
ANCHOR_WALLET=~/.config/solana/id.json \
npx ts-node scripts/initialize_devnet.ts

ANCHOR_PROVIDER_URL="https://api.devnet.solana.com" \
ANCHOR_WALLET=~/.config/solana/id.json \
npx ts-node scripts/open_drift_account.ts

ANCHOR_PROVIDER_URL="https://api.devnet.solana.com" \
ANCHOR_WALLET=~/.config/solana/id.json \
npx ts-node scripts/deposit_into_drift.ts

4. Run the Shadow Weaver executor

# Start the delta-neutral funding arbitrage bot
npx ts-node bot/shadow_weaver.ts

With this process complete, the judge can observe logs, P&L evolution, and funding‑driven trades directly in the terminal and validate corresponding state transitions on Solana and Drift.
